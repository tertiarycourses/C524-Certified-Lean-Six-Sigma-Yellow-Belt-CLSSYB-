# Learner Guide: Certified Lean Six Sigma Yellow Belt (CLSSYB)

## Purpose

This guide supports the C524 Certified Lean Six Sigma Yellow Belt course. It walks you through a practical 2-day sequence of labs aligned to Yellow Belt expectations: understanding quality improvement, identifying Lean waste, mapping processes, supporting root cause and risk analysis, and sustaining process improvements.

Yellow Belt learners are expected to support Green Belt or Black Belt-led projects, contribute observations from the process, help collect data, build simple process maps, participate in root cause discussions, and help implement improvement actions.

## Learning Outcomes

After completing these labs, you will be able to:

- Explain Lean Six Sigma, quality, customer satisfaction, COPQ, DMAIC, sigma, and DPMO at Yellow Belt level.
- Identify value-added and non-value-added work.
- Recognise the 8 wastes of Lean in service and operational processes.
- Build SIPOC, process walk notes, spaghetti diagrams, value stream maps, and action plans.
- Write clear problem statements, select useful metrics, and create SMART goals.
- Use brainstorming, affinity grouping, 5 Whys, fishbone diagrams, and basic FMEA thinking.
- Apply 5S, standard work, visual management, and mistake proofing to improvement opportunities.
- Create a simple control plan and audit checklist to sustain improvements.

## Working Scenario

Use one process throughout the course. If your instructor does not assign one, use this scenario:

**Course registration process**

Learners submit course enquiries and registration requests. The team checks eligibility, confirms course dates, collects payment or sponsorship details, sends confirmation emails, and prepares attendance records. ## Day 1 Flow

### Lab 01: Quality Improvement and Lean Six Sigma Overview

1. Choose your process.
2. Identify the internal or external customer.
3. List customer expectations.
4. Define what quality means for the process.
5. Identify examples of poor quality.
6. Estimate Cost of Poor Quality categories: rework, delay, complaints, extra labour, lost opportunity.
7. Explain how Lean and Six Sigma contribute differently.
8. Summarise the improvement opportunity in one paragraph.

Output: process quality profile and COPQ notes.

### Lab 02: DMAIC, Sigma, and Yellow Belt Project Support

1. Review Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, and Control.
2. Select one improvement opportunity.
3. Identify what a Yellow Belt can support in each DMAIC phase.
4. Define unit, defect, and opportunity for the process.
5. Create a small sample data table.
6. Calculate defect percentage.
7. Explain what DPMO and sigma represent as comparison concepts.
8. Build a Yellow Belt contribution checklist.

Output: DMAIC support map and basic measurement worksheet.

### Lab 03: Lean Thinking, Gemba, and Kaizen

1. Review Lean principles and value from the customer point of view.
2. Plan a Gemba walk for your process.
3. Observe or simulate each major process step.
4. Record facts, waiting points, handoffs, and rework.
5. Identify one Kaizen opportunity.
6. Write a small improvement idea that can be tested safely.

Output: Gemba observation sheet and Kaizen idea.

### Lab 04: Eight Wastes and Value-Added Analysis

1. Review the 8 wastes: transportation, inventory, motion, waiting, overproduction, overprocessing, defects, and skills.
2. Break your process into steps.
3. Classify each step as value-added, business-required, or non-value-added.
4. Identify at least eight waste examples.
5. Prioritise wastes by frequency and impact.
6. Select one high-value waste reduction opportunity.

Output: value-added analysis and waste priority table.

### Lab 05: SIPOC and Process Walk

1. Define the process start and end boundaries.
2. Identify suppliers.
3. Identify inputs.
4. Identify high-level process steps.
5. Identify outputs.
6. Identify customers.
7. Use a process walk to validate whether the SIPOC reflects reality.
8. Update the SIPOC based on observations.

Output: SIPOC diagram and process walk notes.

## Day 2 Flow

### Lab 06: Value Stream Mapping and PICK Action Planning

1. Draw a current-state value stream map.
2. Add process time and wait time where possible.
3. Mark wastes, bottlenecks, and rework loops.
4. Draw a future-state idea.
5. List possible actions.
6. Plot actions on a PICK chart: Possible, Implement, Challenge, Kill.
7. Convert selected items into an action plan.

Output: current-state map, future-state idea, PICK chart, and action plan.

### Lab 07: Problem Statements, Metrics, and SMART Goals

1. Select the highest-priority problem.
2. Write a factual problem statement.
3. Remove blame, causes, and solutions from the statement.
4. Select one primary metric.
5. Select supporting metrics such as time, quality, cost, or customer satisfaction.
6. Write a SMART goal.
7. Check whether the goal is realistic and measurable.

Output: problem statement, metric set, and SMART goal.

### Lab 08: Root Cause, Risk, and Change Readiness

1. Brainstorm possible causes.
2. Group ideas using an affinity diagram.
3. Use 5 Whys on one likely cause.
4. Build a fishbone diagram.
5. Identify risks using a simple FMEA table.
6. Score severity, occurrence, and detection.
7. Choose risk responses.
8. Use Q x A = I to discuss change acceptance.

Output: affinity groups, 5 Whys, fishbone diagram, FMEA table, and change readiness note.

### Lab 09: 5S, Standard Work, Visual Management, and Poka-Yoke

1. Choose one improvement opportunity.
2. Apply 5S: sort, set in order, shine, standardise, sustain.
3. Draft a standard work checklist.
4. Add one visual management idea.
5. Add one mistake-proofing idea.
6. Explain how the solution reduces errors, delays, or rework.

Output: 5S plan, standard work checklist, visual control, and poka-yoke idea.

### Lab 10: Control Plan, Audit, and Yellow Belt Readiness

1. Identify what must be monitored after improvement.
2. Create a control plan.
3. Create an audit checklist.
4. Assign owners and review frequency.
5. Identify warning signs of regression.
6. Complete the readiness checklist.
7. Present your mini improvement storyboard.

Output: control plan, audit checklist, readiness checklist, and final storyboard.

## Final Storyboard Format

| Section | What to Include |
| --- | --- |
| Process | Name, customer, start and end points |
| Problem | Factual problem statement |
| Data | Baseline metric and sample notes |
| Analysis | Waste, root cause, and risk summary |
| Improvement | Selected solution and action plan |
| Control | Control plan, audit method, owner, review frequency |
