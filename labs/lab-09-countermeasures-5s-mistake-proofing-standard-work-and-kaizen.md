# Lab 9 — Countermeasures, 5S, Mistake Proofing, Standard Work, and Kaizen

**DMAIC phase:** IMPROVE  |  **Lab type:** Core  |  **Course:** Certified Lean Six Sigma Yellow Belt (CLSSYB) Training (C524)

## Objective

Recommend improvement actions that address the proven root cause (A5).

## Scenario

Contoso Service Desk handles employee IT requests. Employees complain that tickets take too long to be assigned, that they must keep chasing for status, and that agents apply different definitions of what counts as "resolved". You are supporting the improvement team as a Yellow Belt.

## What you will build

A prioritised countermeasure set with a 5S plan, a poka-yoke design and standard work.

**Tools and techniques:** Brainstorming, 5S, poka-yoke, visual management, standard work, Kaizen

## Steps

### Step 1

Generate countermeasures for each proven root cause — quantity before quality.

### Step 2

Prioritise using an impact vs effort matrix to find the quick wins.

### Step 3

Apply 5S thinking — Sort, Set in order, Shine, Standardise, Sustain — including to digital work.

### Step 4

Design one poka-yoke that makes the error difficult or impossible to make.

### Step 5

Draft standard work so the improved method is repeatable by anyone.

### Step 6

Plan a Kaizen event or pilot to test the countermeasure at small scale first.

### Step 7

Distinguish a containment countermeasure from a permanent solution.

## Check your work

Every countermeasure traces back to a proven root cause, and your poka-yoke prevents rather than detects the error.

## Deliverable

Save your output — it forms part of your Contoso improvement package and is your reference for applying these tools at work.
