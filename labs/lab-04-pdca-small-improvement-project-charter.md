# Lab 4 — PDCA Small Improvement Project Charter

**DMAIC phase:** DEFINE  |  **Lab type:** Elective  |  **Course:** Certified Lean Six Sigma Yellow Belt (CLSSYB) Training (C524)

> **Elective lab.** Complete this lab if time allows during class, or afterwards as additional practice. It extends the same Contoso Service Desk scenario used by the core labs.

## Objective

Charter a small improvement using PDCA with a measurable goal (A1, A2).

## Scenario

Contoso Service Desk handles employee IT requests. Employees complain that tickets take too long to be assigned, that they must keep chasing for status, and that agents apply different definitions of what counts as "resolved". You are supporting the improvement team as a Yellow Belt.

## What you will build

A one-page PDCA charter with problem statement, goal, scope and success measure.

**Tools and techniques:** PDCA cycle, problem/goal statements, scope in-out, stakeholder list

## Steps

### Step 1

Map the four PDCA phases to concrete actions for your scenario.

### Step 2

Write the problem statement: process, time period, measurable issue and impact — no solution.

### Step 3

Write a measurable goal statement: metric, baseline, target and date.

### Step 4

Define scope with an explicit in-scope / out-of-scope table to prevent scope creep.

### Step 5

Identify stakeholders and the decision you will make after the Check phase.

### Step 6

Confirm the improvement is small enough to test within two weeks.

## Check your work

Your goal statement contains a metric, a baseline, a target and a date, and your problem statement names no solution.

## Deliverable

Save your output — it forms part of your Contoso improvement package and is your reference for applying these tools at work.
