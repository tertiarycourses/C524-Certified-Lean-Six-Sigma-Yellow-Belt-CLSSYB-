# Lab 5 — DMAIC Overview, Problem Statement, Scope, and Stakeholders

**DMAIC phase:** DEFINE  |  **Lab type:** Core  |  **Course:** Certified Lean Six Sigma Yellow Belt (CLSSYB) Training (C524)

## Objective

Support the Define phase of a DMAIC project (A1, A2).

## Scenario

Contoso Service Desk handles employee IT requests. Employees complain that tickets take too long to be assigned, that they must keep chasing for status, and that agents apply different definitions of what counts as "resolved". You are supporting the improvement team as a Yellow Belt.

## What you will build

A DMAIC phase table, refined problem statement, stakeholder map and benefit estimate.

**Tools and techniques:** DMAIC roadmap, project charter, stakeholder analysis, CTQ linkage

## Steps

### Step 1

Summarise all five DMAIC phases: purpose, key deliverable and Yellow Belt support role.

### Step 2

Refine the problem statement so it is specific, measurable and solution-free.

### Step 3

Identify stakeholders and classify each by influence and interest.

### Step 4

Define the project scope and state the expected benefit in business terms.

### Step 5

Link the problem back to the CTQ requirements captured in Lab 2.

### Step 6

Confirm which DMAIC phases a Yellow Belt can support most strongly.

## Check your work

Your problem statement passes the 'no solution named' test and every stakeholder has a defined engagement approach.

## Deliverable

Save your output — it forms part of your Contoso improvement package and is your reference for applying these tools at work.
