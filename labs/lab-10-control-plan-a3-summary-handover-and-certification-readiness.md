# Lab 10 — Control Plan, A3 Summary, Handover, and Certification Readiness

**DMAIC phase:** CONTROL  |  **Lab type:** Core  |  **Course:** Certified Lean Six Sigma Yellow Belt (CLSSYB) Training (C524)

## Objective

Recommend control actions to sustain process performance (A5).

## Scenario

Contoso Service Desk handles employee IT requests. Employees complain that tickets take too long to be assigned, that they must keep chasing for status, and that agents apply different definitions of what counts as "resolved". You are supporting the improvement team as a Yellow Belt.

## What you will build

A control plan, an A3 one-page summary, a handover checklist and a readiness plan.

**Tools and techniques:** Control plan, SPC thinking, visual management, A3 report, handover

## Steps

### Step 1

Create the control plan: metric, target, monitoring method, frequency, owner, reaction plan.

### Step 2

Define the visual management approach — board, dashboard or huddle — that keeps it visible.

### Step 3

Specify the reaction plan: exactly what happens when a metric falls outside target.

### Step 4

Write the A3 summary: background, current state, goal, analysis, countermeasures, results, follow-up.

### Step 5

Prepare the handover checklist so the process owner can sustain it without you.

### Step 6

Complete your personal certification readiness plan — which topics need most review.

## Check your work

Every control plan row has a named owner and a reaction plan, and your A3 fits on one page.

## Deliverable

Save your output — it forms part of your Contoso improvement package and is your reference for applying these tools at work.
