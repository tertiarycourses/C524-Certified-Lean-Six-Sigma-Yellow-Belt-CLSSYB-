# Lab 3 — SIPOC, Process Mapping, Handoffs, and SME Support

**DMAIC phase:** DEFINE  |  **Lab type:** Core  |  **Course:** Certified Lean Six Sigma Yellow Belt (CLSSYB) Training (C524)

## Objective

Map a process with SIPOC and a detailed process map to expose handoffs (A2, A3).

## Scenario

Contoso Service Desk handles employee IT requests. Employees complain that tickets take too long to be assigned, that they must keep chasing for status, and that agents apply different definitions of what counts as "resolved". You are supporting the improvement team as a Yellow Belt.

## What you will build

A completed SIPOC and a detailed process map with pain points marked.

**Tools and techniques:** SIPOC, process flowchart, swimlane map, standard process symbols

## Steps

### Step 1

Set the process boundaries — agree the explicit start and stop points first.

### Step 2

Build the SIPOC: Suppliers, Inputs, Process (5-7 high-level steps), Outputs, Customers.

### Step 3

Expand into a detailed process map: Step, Actor, Activity, System, Handoff.

### Step 4

Draw the same flow as a swimlane map so each actor's lane makes handoffs obvious.

### Step 5

Mark pain points — delays, rework loops, unclear ownership and handoff risks.

### Step 6

Prepare SME notes for the Green Belt: what you observed and what needs validation.

## Check your work

Your SIPOC has all five columns populated and every handoff on the detailed map has a named owner on both sides.

## Deliverable

Save your output — it forms part of your Contoso improvement package and is your reference for applying these tools at work.
